Frequently Asked Questions

See below some answers to common questions that I have been asked over the years.

What can I expect in the first counselling session?

We will briefly discuss the purpose of your visit, and an overview of the counseling process. We can talk about your background, current challenges, and goals for therapy if you wish. You'll also have an opportunity to assess if my attributes and personality are a good fit for you and if you feel comfortable.

How does mentoring differ from counselling?

Mentoring and counselling differ in their primary focus, goals, and the nature of the relationship. Mentoring emphasizes guidance and development, leveraging experience to help someone achieve career or personal goals. Counselling, on the other hand, focuses on addressing emotional, psychological, or behavioral challenges through talking therapy.

How long is one mentoring or counselling session?

On average, most sessions last for an hour but it truly depends on the client's own preference and what they believe is worthwhile (either shorter or longer).

Does mindfulness really work?

It can be beneficial for many people. Research suggests it can be effective in managing stress, anxiety, and depression, and may also have positive effects on physical health. However, it's not a one-size-fits-all solution, everyone can have a different point of view but I can say that it has been transformative for my own life.
